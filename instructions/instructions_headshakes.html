HEAD SHAKES

  • A head shake is a repeated rotation of the head from one side to the other. IT IS AS IF THE PERSON IS SAYING "no..." or "not good..." and shaking his/her head left to right.
  • A head shake MUST have AT LEAST 2 continuous directional movements (head moving LEFT first and then RIGHT or moving from RIGHT first and then LEFT). A headshake can be very SUBTLE!
  • For each head shake, do your best to identify the EXACT starting frame and the ending frame.
  • Identify ALL instances of head shakes.
  • If you are not quite sure whether it is a head shake or not, DON'T annotate.
  • Have the sound on. It can help.
